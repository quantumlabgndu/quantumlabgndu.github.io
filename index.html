Welcome to the Quantum Lab

Quantum Sensing Quantum Computing & Simulation Custom Scientific Instruments Physics Education Innovation

We are a hands-on, ideas-to-hardware group advancing quantum technologies and physics education. Our work spans sensitive quantum sensors, simulation-driven quantum computing, in-house instrumentation, and classroom innovation—all to push the frontiers of discovery and learning.

Quantum Sensing (NV, SiC, Organics)

We build room-temperature quantum sensors using NV centers in diamond, silicon vacancies in SiC, and photo-excited triplet states in doped organic crystals. These platforms enable ultra-sensitive measurements of magnetic fields, temperature, and pressure for materials, biology, and beyond.

Quantum Computing & Simulation

We explore quantum algorithms on IBM Quantum and on a simulation-based NMR quantum information processor. A core thrust is simulating nuclear and chemical Hamiltonians for chemistry, materials, and many-body physics—bridging theory with practical quantum workflows.

Instruments We Build

From concept to circuit, we design and fabricate our own scientific instruments: custom RF chains, FPGA-based control, precision electronics, and experimental tooling. This lets us iterate fast, unlock new measurements, and keep our science agile and affordable.

Physics Education, Upgraded

We integrate modern technologies into physics experiments to deepen conceptual understanding and engagement. By reimagining labs and demonstrations with sensors, data, and interactivity, we help students experience physics as something they do, not just read.

Research Projects

Quantum Sensing

Our research in quantum sensing leverages the quantum properties of spin defects to achieve ultra-sensitive measurements of magnetic fields, temperature, and pressure. We focus on solid-state systems such as silicon vacancy centers in silicon carbide and photoexcited triplet states in pentacene-doped organic crystals, which operate effectively even under ambient conditions.

By engineering and controlling these quantum defects, we aim to develop scalable, room-temperature quantum sensors for applications ranging from nanoscale magnetic resonance and materials characterization to biological sensing.

Quantum Machine Learning Algorithms

This research area explores the intersection of quantum computing and artificial intelligence. We develop and analyze quantum algorithms that can offer significant speedups for machine learning tasks.

Fault-Tolerant Quantum Error Correction

We design and analyze quantum error-correcting codes (surface and color codes), studying thresholds, resources, and efficient decoders for fault-tolerant quantum computation.
